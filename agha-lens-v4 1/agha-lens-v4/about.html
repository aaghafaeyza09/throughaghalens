✦   Origin   ✦
The Beginning

It started with curiosity,
and never really stopped.

Photography found me during my junior high school years — a period when the urge to understand the visual world first began to stir. It started simply: a desire to capture moments, to hold onto things before they slipped away.

But somewhere along the way, the act of taking pictures became something deeper. It became a way of seeing — of looking at the world not just as it appears, but as it feels.

✦   Philosophy   ✦
Why Landscape & Nature

Because nature is the only thing
that is truly honest.

There is a stillness in landscape that cannot be manufactured. The mist settling on a hillside at dawn. Light filtering through a forest canopy in slow, golden shafts. A sky that transforms in silence, shifting through colors that have no names.

These moments do not perform for the camera. They simply exist — and in that existence, they carry a weight that I am endlessly drawn to. Landscape is not just a subject. It is a feeling. And that is precisely where I fell in love.

"

Photography is not about what we see —
it is about what we feel.

— Agha Faeyza
